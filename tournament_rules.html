‚öñÔ∏èGroup Stage Tiebreaker Rules

If teams are tied on points for a Grand Final spot, the following tiebreaker matches will be played.

  • Two-Team Tie

    If two teams are tied for a qualifying spot, they will play a single Best-of-One (Bo1) decider match. The winner qualifies.

  • Three-Team Tie (for 2 spots)

    A two-match series is played. Seeding (X, Y, Z) is determined by tiebreaker criteria (Game Differential, etc.).

    • Match 1 (Bo1): Team X vs. Team Y. Winner qualifies.
    • Match 2 (Bo1): Loser of Match 1 vs. Team Z. Winner qualifies.

  • Three-Team Tie (for 1 spot)

    A two-stage gauntlet is played, seeded 1, 2, and 3.

    • Match 1 (Bo1): Seed 2 vs. Seed 3. Loser is eliminated.
    • Match 2 (Bo1): Winner of Match 1 vs. Seed 1. Winner qualifies.

  • Four-Team Tie (for 2 spots)

    Two simultaneous semifinal matches are played, seeded 1-4.

    • Semifinal 1 (Bo1): Seed 1 vs. Seed 4.
    • Semifinal 2 (Bo1): Seed 2 vs. Seed 3.
    • The winners of both semifinals qualify for the Grand Final.

‚ö†Ô∏èAdministrative Notes

  • Tiebreaker matches do not alter the final group stage statistics (e.g., game differential).
  • For all Bo1 tiebreakers, side choice (Radiant/Dire) or first-pick priority will be given to the team with the better tiebreaker stats. If all stats are equal, it will be decided by a coin toss.
  • The tournament administration reserves the right to adjust seeding methods to fit schedule constraints, but any changes will be announced before play begins.

üîÑDouble Elimination Format

This format provides every team with a second chance. No team is eliminated after a single match loss.

  • Starting Point: All teams begin in the Upper Bracket.
  • First Loss: If a team loses in the Upper Bracket, they are not eliminated. Instead, they drop down to the Lower Bracket.

Before each match, teams will perform a map veto to determine the three maps that will be played. The process is as follows:

üé≤What is Ludo?

Ludo is a classic strategy board game where players race their four tokens from their starting point to the center "home" triangle. Moves are determined by the roll of a single die. The objective is to be the first to get all four of your tokens home. In Team Up mode, you'll work with your partner to secure victory together!

‚ö°Power Mode Explained

Power Mode adds an extra layer of strategy with special items you can use to gain an advantage:

  • Double Distance: Doubles the value of your next dice roll.
  • Dice Control: Allows you to choose the exact number you want on the die.
  • Protection Shield: Protects your token from being captured for one turn.

The King of Iron Fist Tournament

Prepare for the next battle. The ultimate test of skill and fighting spirit awaits.

üèÜTournament Structure

  • Total Players: 16
  • Format: 1 vs 1 (Individual)
  • Bracket Style: Double Elimination
  • Match Format: All matches leading to the Grand Final will be Best of 3 Games (BO3).

üìúCore Rules

  1. Character Lock: Each player must select one character for the entire tournament. You cannot switch characters between matches or games. Choose wisely!
  2. Side Selection: Players may mutually agree on who sits on the 1P or 2P side. If no agreement can be reached, a coin toss will determine the sides.
  3. Punctuality: Be present at your station when your match is called. Failure to appear within 5 minutes may result in a forfeit of the match.

üîÑDouble Elimination Explained

This format ensures every player gets a second chance. No one is eliminated after a single loss.

  • Two Lives: Every player starts in the Winners Bracket. If you lose a match, you drop to the Losers Bracket.
  • Elimination: If you lose a match while in the Losers Bracket, you are eliminated from the tournament.
  • Two Paths to Glory: The tournament proceeds until one player remains in the Winners Bracket (the "Winners Finalist") and one player remains in the Losers Bracket (the "Losers Finalist").

üëëThe Grand Final

The Grand Final is the ultimate showdown between the Winners Finalist and the Losers Finalist. This match is Best of 5 Games (BO5).

  • The player from the Winners Bracket only needs to win one set (BO5) to be crowned the champion.
  • The player from the Losers Bracket must win two consecutive sets (BO5) to become the champion. This is known as a Bracket Reset.
  • Why the Bracket Reset? The Winners Finalist has not lost a match yet. To be eliminated, they must be defeated twice. If the Losers Finalist wins the first BO5 set, the bracket is "reset," and both players effectively have one "loss." A final, decisive BO5 set is then played to determine the true King of the Iron Fist.

Good luck, fighters. Show us your strength!
